Early Signs of Heart Disease

By Dr. Sarah Johnson, Cardiologist · Jan 15, 2026

Heart disease remains one of the leading causes of death worldwide. Recognizing early warning signs can lead to earlier diagnosis and better outcomes.

Common early symptoms

Chest discomfort (pressure, tightness, or pain), shortness of breath—especially with activity—fatigue, and swelling in the legs or ankles can signal heart issues. Some people experience jaw or back pain, nausea, or lightheadedness.

When to see a cardiologist

If you have risk factors (family history, high blood pressure, diabetes, smoking, obesity) or notice any of these symptoms regularly, it’s important to get evaluated. A cardiologist can run tests such as ECG, stress tests, or echocardiograms to assess your heart health.

Don’t wait for a crisis. Early intervention can help manage conditions like hypertension and coronary artery disease with lifestyle changes and medication.
