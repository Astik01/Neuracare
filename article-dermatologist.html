When to See a Dermatologist

By Dr. Michael Chen, Dermatologist · Jan 10, 2026

Your skin can signal everything from minor irritation to serious conditions. Knowing when to see a dermatologist helps you get the right care at the right time.

Skin changes to watch for

New or changing moles (asymmetry, irregular borders, color variation, diameter larger than a pencil eraser), persistent rashes, unexplained itching, open sores that don’t heal, or sudden acne in adults warrant a professional evaluation.

Common reasons to book a visit

Acne that doesn’t improve with over-the-counter products, eczema or psoriasis flare-ups, hair loss, nail changes, or any lesion that bleeds, crusts, or grows. Skin cancer screening is also recommended for at-risk individuals or annual check-ups.

A dermatologist can diagnose and recommend treatment—from topical creams to procedures—tailored to your condition.
